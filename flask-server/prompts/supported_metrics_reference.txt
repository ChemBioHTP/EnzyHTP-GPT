    - Electric Field Strength along the Bond
        + JSON Name:
            electric_field
        + Description:
            the electric field strength of the internal electric field of an enzyme along a target bond at the center of the bond.
        + Required argument:
            - atom_1: the first atom of the bond of interest
            - atom_2: the second atom of the bond of interest
            NOTE: The format of atom_1, atom_2 is "Chain_id.Residue_index.Atom_name"
            Example: "A.125.CA" - means the CA atom in residue 125 of chain A
        + Optional argument:
            N/A
        + Related properties (under different MD models)
            (NOTE: The related properties are different under different MD models)
            - MD Model: protein-ligand complex without constraint
                + Related property: Electric Field, Activation Barrier, Enzyme Activity
            - MD Model: pre-reaction complex (protein-ligand complex with a constraint applied to the "forming bond")
                + Related property: Electric Field, Activation Barrier, Enzyme Activity
            - MD Model: apo-protein without constraint
                + Related property: None

    - Substrate Positioning Index (SPI)
        + JSON Name:
            spi
        + Description:
            the substrate positioning index is defined by the ratio between the solvent-accessible surface area (SASA) of the substrate and the SASA of the enzyme pocket.
            Studies by the Yang group at Vanderbilt show that it correlates with the enzyme activity that a favorable SPI corresponds to a larger proportion of reactive conformations.
        + Required argument:
            - substrate_selection_pattern: the PyMol style selection pattern defining the substrate. Refer to *PyMol selection pattern*
        + Optional argument:
            - pocket_selection_pattern: the PyMol style selection pattern defining the pocket. (by default residues 5 angstroms near the substrate) Refer to *PyMol selection pattern*
        + Related Properties 
            (NOTE: The related properties are different under different MD models)
            - MD Model: protein-ligand complex without constraint
                + Related property: None
            - MD Model: pre-reaction complex (protein-ligand complex with a constraint applied to the "forming bond")
                + Related property: Substrate Positioning Dynamics, Activation Barrier, Enzyme Activity
            - MD Model: apo-protein without constraint
                + Related property: None

    - Active Site RMSD
        + JSON Name:
            active_site_rmsd
        + Description:
            Active site RMSD is defined as the average of the frame-by-frame root-mean-square deviation of all heavy-atom coordinates of residues that composes the active site of the enzyme relative to an average structure, calculated over the entire molecular-dynamics trajectory.
        + Required argument:
            - pocket_selection_pattern: the PyMol style selection pattern defining the active site pocket. Refer to *PyMol selection pattern* (by default 5 angstroms near the substrate if the substrate is given)
        + Optional argument:
            - None
        + Related Properties 
            (NOTE: The related properties are different under different MD models)
            - MD Model: protein-ligand complex without constraint
                + Related property: Substrate Positioning Dynamics, Activation Barrier, Enzyme Activity, Ligand binding
            - MD Model: pre-reaction complex (protein-ligand complex with a constraint applied to the "forming bond")
                + Related properties: Substrate Positioning Dynamics, Activation Barrier, Enzyme Activity, Ligand binding
            - MD Model: apo-protein without constraint
                + Related property: Substrate Positioning Dynamics, Activation Barrier, Enzyme Activity, Ligand binding

    - Domain Seperation Index (DSI)
        + JSON Name:
            dsi
        + Description:
            The domain seperation index of the two domains of a bidomain enzyme. This index describes how seperate the two domains are in the dynamic motion of the enzyme.
            A study by Ning et al showed it is predictive for the cold-adaption ability (the ability to maintain activity at lower temperature) of bidomain enzymes. 
        + Required argument:
            - linker_sequence: the sequence of the linker. (use to define the sequence range of the two domains)
        + Optional argument:
            - None
        + Related Properties 
            (NOTE: The related properties are different under different MD models)
            - MD Model: protein-ligand complex without constraint
                + Related property: None
            - MD Model: pre-reaction complex (protein-ligand complex with a constraint applied to the "forming bond")
                + Related property: None
            - MD Model: apo-protein without constraint
                + Related property: Enzyme Activity Change as Temperature Change, Cold-adaption Ability, Enzyme Activity at a lower temperature relative to a higher temperature
